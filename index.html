How I Work

A simple support process that keeps users informed and systems cared for.

1. Listen & Clarify

I start by understanding the issue from the userâ€™s point of view. I ask clear, friendly questions and repeat back key details to confirm what is going wrong.

Output: A simple summary of the problem and what we will check first.

2. Check the Basics

Before going deep, I check simple things: logins, permissions, connections, recent changes, and tool status. This saves time and keeps users from feeling rushed.

Output: Quick wins where possible and a list of next checks.

3. Troubleshoot & Test

I follow a clear path: reproduce the issue, try one change at a time, test again, and keep notes. Communication stays simple and honest at every step.

Output: Tested steps and a record of what worked or did not work.

4. Document & Share

Once we have a fix or workaround, I write it in clear language so other team members and future users can benefit from the same solution.

Output: Simple guides, notes, and updated docs.

5. Close the Loop

I confirm with the user that things are working again and check how they feel about the experience. Where needed, I suggest small improvements to prevent the issue from returning.

Output: A user who feels heard, informed, and supported.

About Joanna

I am building my career in tech with a strong base in customer service and a growing focus on IT support and CRM. I enjoy solving problems, breaking tasks into simple steps, and making sure people feel guided, not lost.

My learning journey includes online courses, live classes, and real practice with tools like Google Drive, Dropbox, Loom, Slack, and Microsoft Teams. I have also completed training such as "Introduction to Data Analysis Using Excel" and use those skills to keep information clean and easy to read.

I believe that good IT support is not just about fixing things. It is about ongoing care, clear updates, and making technology feel lighter for the user. I bring patience, structure, and a steady attitude to every request I handle.

What You Can Expect From Me:

  • I listen first, ask clear questions, and summarize the problem before touching any tool.
  • I write in simple language so any teammate can understand what was done and why.
  • I keep records neat, from CRM notes to support tickets and checklists.
  • I treat every user with respect, no matter how small or big the request feels.
  • I take learning seriously and keep improving my technical and people skills.

If you need someone who can grow with your team, support your tools, and care about the small details that shape the customer experience, I would be glad to work with you.

Common Questions

A few quick answers about working with me for IT Support and CRM-related roles.

What kind of roles are you open to?

I am open to entry-level and junior roles in IT Support, Help Desk, CRM support, customer support, and internal tools support. I am also happy to help with part-time or contract work around documentation and process support.

Do you work remotely?

Yes. I am comfortable working fully remote using tools like Slack, Microsoft Teams, Zoom, and cloud-based platforms. I keep my communication clear and responsive so remote work feels smooth.

How do you handle learning new tools?

I learn by reading docs, watching short demos, and practicing with real examples. I take notes and create simple guides so I can remember steps and help others use the same tools later.

How do you manage your time during busy days?

I group tasks by urgency and impact, keep a simple list of open tickets or requests, and update people often so they know where things stand. I also keep space for short breaks so I stay focused.

Can you help improve our existing support process?

Yes. I can review how tickets, notes, and tools are being used today, then suggest small changes like better templates, clearer tags, or shorter steps that make life easier for both users and the team.

How soon can you start?

I am flexible and can start quickly for most roles or projects. We can discuss your timeline, onboarding needs, and tools during our first conversation.
